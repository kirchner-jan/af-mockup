The Book of Why is, as far as I know, the only sourcebook in the world devoted solely to textbooks. This is the one that explains to first-dan black belt why exactly the two jiu-jitsu techniques work, explains the relationship between nutrition and muscle loss, goes into greater detail on the causes of violence, and explains how to use a computer's command line.


It's everything a good textbook should be\xe2\x80\x94and everything an encyclopedia, or even a textbook on a narrow subject, should not be.


It is also an extraordinary project. I will leave many questions unanswered. I will tell you what The Book of Now _does not cover, and what _The Book doesn't cover. Some of the books I recommend below have not been published for more than ten years, but even with that fact in mind, the quality remains. I will say what I consider an important fact regarding some of the books below without telling you if that's actually a fact or a speculation, although I will occasionally give away a fact.


I have tried my hardest to link to some of the more notable textbooks below, but there are also some smaller books below which I haven't been able to link to, let me know and I'll try to add them if I can figure out what the appropriate URLs are.


The vast majority of the books below are on the hard science of human health. A few cover the basics of business, and business books are better than textbooks of anything else. All of these are organized by subject and by general type, which means some books are very technical and contain equations or graphs, while others summarize, clarify, or explain, which is why I've included textbooks on psychology, economics, and sociology in those sections as well.


The list below is not intended to be an endorsement of everything below, I just want to link to it so that you see what all I've tried to do.

Nutrition


These books all cover an awful lot of material, even if many of them have gone into print only recently.


  • The Science of Winning at Life: A Revolutionary Guide for Dieting the Easy Way to Lose 20 Pounds or More\xe2\x80\x94For everyone who's tried dieting before.

  • The Science of Health and Lifestyle Nutrition\xe2\x80\x94The easy way to lose up to 20 pounds in eight weeks. No calorie counting, cutting, limiting or restricting. The secrets of how we get to lose weight\xe2\x80\x94how we eat less fat, how we eat less carbohydrate, and how we eat less cholesterol. No sugar, no artificial sugar, no chemical preservatives, no processed foods, no carbs at night, no carbs too often and no carbs at all most of the time.

  • Nutrient Timings\xe2\x80\x94the book that taught me what to eat to optimize my own health... and which of my old ideas were just plain wrong.

  • Calories: A Guide to Everything\xe2\x80\x94A detailed guide to everything that's relevant to nutrition, including calorie intake, macronutrients, micronutrients, how to lose weight, which foods are the best... and probably about 30 other subjects no one will ever hear about.

  • How Your Body Uses Fat and Carbohydrates\xe2\x80\x94A comprehensive study of the ways the body uses fats and carbohydrates. Based on a review of the major scientific studies on the health effects of fats and carbohydrates.

  • Eating Healthy, Dieting Easier\xe2\x80\x94The dieting books I read before I understood why calories aren't very important. This one is aimed at helping dieters eat healthily and lose weight.

If you are interested in anything covered in the above books I would love to hear from you whether or not you read them.

Psychology


If something does not seem to fit in with any of the above books, please do comment. But I think they are a really good place to start (unless you have a subject you particularly care about, in which case maybe you should skip the links and read the specific books).


  • The Minds of the Bedroom\xe2\x80\x94A very interesting look at how our minds form, as well as our sexual development.

  • The Woman's Bible: Old and New Testaments Reveal God's Truth About Marriage\xe2\x80\x94An old-and-new-spin on the Bible tells women the men in their lives don't always understand how their decisions affect them.

  • The Evolution Of Human Sexuality\xe2\x80\x94A brief overview of human sexuality, from the different human preferences (boredom vs excitement vs intimacy vs variety) to the most common causes of infidelity (boredom, a sense of meaninglessness, or an unconscious effort to experiment with new partners).

  • Evolutionary Psychology: 4th Edition\xe2\x80\x94A good summary of evolutionary psychology by Robert Wright, including: why we evolved to be attracted to partners who are similar to us, why there is a difference between men and women, and why gender differences in behavior and in the brain are due to natural selection.

Buddpsych-Socrates is mostly focused on Buddhism, but may be a good start for the general rationalist community interested in buddhist thought. I think he is more interesting, but much less definitive in his arguments and is therefore unlikely to give many of his views as hard take-aways.
