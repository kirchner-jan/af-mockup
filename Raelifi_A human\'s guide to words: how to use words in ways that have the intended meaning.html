I've been trying to understand why I've been having so much trouble understanding some people's insistence that the "meaning" of words is something more to them than the specific letter-and-spacings printed on the inside of their skull.


I've come up with the following solution:


A _word _is nothing. A _meaning _is the purpose for which a person created a word.


What people who insist the meaning of words is different from the letter-and-spaces inside their skull misunderstand is that they're trying to read into the word meanings the structure, order, and purpose of the words themselves.


They're reading "the meaning of" and "in what sense can" as though they were the nouns in a sentence and are to be found in the subject. It's not because they want to give their interpretations of the meanings of words, or use them to argue from semantics to metasemantics, but because they think that meanings are somehow more _meaningful _than the meanings are.


And the reason that they think that is because the letters and spaces of a word are immutable, whereas the meaning isn't. They believe they can change a word by, I don't know, adding a word or swapping out a letter\xe2\x80\x94some strange, weird belief they carry around for just that reason, because meaning's supposed to be mutable but not letter-and-space.


But words are really just made of letters and spaces, like any other sequence of letters and spaces. If I type the letters "mean" you have now received the word "mean". This is the same thing as typing "bog" and getting out "bog". If that letter that just showed up wasn't there, I can't be "out of the bog".


The only difference is that meanings are more like a determined purpose as words are. Like a word meaning is a purpose as a bog is a determined amount of water.


And word-meanings, purposes, are not like the internal state they are created in. Meaning-permanence is not like the impermanence of internal states because even permanency is a determined purpose just as purposefulness is by definition.


To understand the word meaning as being created by the particular purpose for which the word is used in is the root of the problem with them. If they _don't know _their words don't _mean _anything _specific _then what's the point of trying to use them instead of a dictionary?
