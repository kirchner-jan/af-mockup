After further reflection, I have changed my mind. It's not that what you see when you look at Islam, it really does change your entire way of seeing.

You shouldn't just take stories and use them to draw inferences about the book. The book is being written right now.

And I just don't know what wrong with my _mind. And furthermore, given the way _Islam is written on TV news, I'm not even tempted to try to imagine what a fundamentalist response to it would be; it's not that easy.

As with all of my previous post "Thoughts on Cryonics", this is still my thought as I think: I would feel the same way if I were a Muslim radicalist myself. This is not some kind of fanatic zeal beyond the pale; it is my genuine opinion.


As I've said before, I do not disagree with any of these people on their fundamental beliefs about rationality and the importance of careful reasoning. I just do not agree that the Islamic part is unimportant.

I was raised to be honest about my beliefs. I've been honest about my beliefs because I've found that, for me, it works to be honest about them. I cannot possibly take a side in the question until I know the answers, and then honestly decide that I honestly agree with both sides.
