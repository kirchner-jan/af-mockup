In this article I will show you how to solve a problem that stumped for millennia the world's most prominent mathematicians. The problem is a famous open question in number theory: Is every perfect number divisible by either 3 or 5?


The most famous proof was given by the Chinese mathematician Bai Juyi in the 12th century AD: the answer is that either a number is divisible by 3 or it is divisible by 5.


By algebra, we can prove that if 3 divides any integer, then 15 also divides it; or, if 5 divides any integer, so does 10. But all integers have either 3 as a divisor or 5 as a divisitor. Therefore 3 is a divisor of an integer if and only if the integer is divisible by 6. This follows from the law of least squares because 3 is not a multiple of 6 but 6 is. Hence neither 3 nor 5 divides an integer if and therefore if the integer is a perfect number.
