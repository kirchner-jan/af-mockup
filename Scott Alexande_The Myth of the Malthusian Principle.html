There's this idea going around that everyone wants to make everyone else do horrible things, and that when they fail to make everyone else help them out, that makes them feel guilty. Why do people think everyone wants to make others do horrible things?"


I can think of two reasons. First: the fear of punishment. They are a consequentialist; if you don't help your fellow man, then by their reasoning, they'll hurt you.So they think they'll be hurt by someone doing something to the poor? And they won't take it from the hands of someone who did it to them? They won't let a rich person do it? Then the rich person ought to go and help the poor man.


Second: the fear of abandonment. If you are a social altruist\xe2\x80\x94if you believe everyone is your equal, and therefore if two people are having trouble one of them owes the other something\xe2\x80\x94then if you don't make them take care of you, they'll be a burden on them.So they think, "The poor man is struggling, and the rich don't look after him, so he does not deserve the help of the rich. He did not want to work hard enough and therefore deserves to starve."


And in fact the latter is the correct logical conclusion, given the correct rules of thumb and the correct emotional responses. This is what most people mean by the Malthusiansphere and why they believe that a rich person helping the poor man is "selfish".


The correct solution to the problem, of course, is the other way around. You are allowed to help the poor man, and the poor man is not allowed to take advantage of your generosity. The rich man is not your equal. But this is a pretty radical conclusion, even compared to the typical leftist position.


Imagine a society where the standard economic theory is correct: The rich are very strong, the middle class are average people getting by, and the poor aren't getting anywhere in life.The standard view of the wealthy is that they are lazy. Now, you might think, the Malthus-consequence argument doesn't actually work as stated here.But it illustrates a larger principle. If you think all men should be equal, that means if two people are struggling and one of them is rich and strong, then the rich person ought rightfully to help the poor person out.They shouldn't be a billionaire while the poor person is barely able to survive.The rich person doesn't work hard enough to be able to take care of themselves, and if they did work hard that would still mean we owed it to the poor person to help them do it.But we don't think all men should have equal resources.
