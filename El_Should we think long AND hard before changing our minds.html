The post is about changing your mind and thinking longer about that decision:


Does reasoning well and quickly help you? Or does it take time to reason and make sure that you are in the right territory.


For example, you have to change your mind if you have information that updates the model you hold. It might be relevant later, and so taking time to think hard changes it.


Or it might not be relevant yet, but it could be relevant down the line. So changing it now changes it.


Or it is relevant and useful in another context. So again, changing it now changes its context. If you have a lot of context, changing a lot could be an issue. So the same kind of situation could have different effects in different contexts.


Can it improve your decisions (if and when) people disagree with you?


In many instances of disagreeing with someone who is an expert in their field and they have a lot of information that you do not, you are basically wrong.


People have lived it and you don't know about it. If the context of that situation is not relevant in that other person's expertise and how good they are at thinking then you are just wrong.


In the case of science, you will disagree with most scientists about facts that they know more than you do. If the facts in question relate to the question you most want to know that is a good thing.


If you have a disagreement with someone who is the leader of a country then you are probably wrong about something pretty important, but that is something you can try to get help about.


But if you have an issue with someone being the leader and they are not being the leader correctly they probably know more than you, but that doesn't mean you are wrong about anything.


In most places in the world, most of the times most people disagree about what decision-relevant facts about a decision-relevant thing are not things that most people know more about than you. That's probably a good reason to doubt your conclusion.


On the contrary, some important decisions and beliefs require having the time to think and gather information from many different angles, including a long time for very complex cases where everything is relevant, and also time for other processes.


For some things it is better to get multiple views and opinions to get the right answer. And for some things it is more important to get it right the first time and hold yourself to a higher standard.
