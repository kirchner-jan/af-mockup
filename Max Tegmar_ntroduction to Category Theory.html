When you look at the word 'Category' in either the standard 'computer science' sense, or in its many other definitions, there is an important question to ask. What does the word mean?


[Note: In the following, the word 'Category', or 'Objective Category Theory' will be used to refer to Category Theory from the standard mathematical point of view. This corresponds to roughly the last quarter century, and is largely the result of work by Steve Awodey and his followers.]


At times, this post will use 'Objective Category Theorists' who mean the people who believe that you cannot speak of mathematical objects without also talking in terms of concepts for objects and morphisms. These people are correct, in my opinion. It's just that their approach is unnecessarily mathematically abstract. All you really need to know about mathematical objects for most applications of mathematics, in my opinion, are either axioms, or properties that a formal system can prove about mathematical objects. So let's talk about structures for the fun of it.
