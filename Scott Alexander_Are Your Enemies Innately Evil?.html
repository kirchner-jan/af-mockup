Are Your Enemies Innately Evil? by Scott Alexander 24 min read 2nd Mar 2022 0 comment Neuromorphic AI + Add Tag 7

B and C may be acting entirely in self-defense; the real problem might be A, who hates everyone who doesn't believe in religion. However, the A-B problem can also happen when B and C have a common enemy, let's call him D. B believes that religion is dangerous and stupid, and C that religion is correct. And D believes that the same of B and C. All of them are united under the banner "No Religion!"


We call C and D "religions neutral," and anyone on either side of the A-B debate is allowed a free speech pass to freely insult anyone else who disagrees with their side—except religions-neutral. I think this is too weak a condemnation, in effect saying: "Anyone who supports A is allowed to insult anyone who supports B, but anyone who supports B is allowed to insult everyone who supports C".


Now, does anyone here have any ideas how we can make this system work at all? Suppose we make it so that nobody will ever be allowed to insult a religion-neutral person. Maybe we ban anyone who has ever used the words "religion-neutral" in their writing or speech—no matter how reasonable and sensible they were claiming to be.


If this works, it would solve all three problems without changing the underlying conflict very much. It might even allow C and D to start insulting A and B in the same way as in their original formulation.


On the other hand, in practice the people who have to deal with A and B won't be able to insult C and D anymore. We get the advantage of no religion-neutral people getting insulted by A and B, without having to sacrifice any of either A's or B's free speech.


So... are religions neutral or are they all enemies? Is there anything we can do to get the advantages of only non-neutral people being allowed to be insulted, without the disadvantages of making everyone involved stop being insulted?
