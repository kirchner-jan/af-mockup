A few weeks ago, I began working (sabbatiously) on a proposal to change my life. I knew almost immediately that it wasn't the right plan, so I ended up doing minimal work on it and decided to forget about it. It's now been over a month since I first became interested in long-term thinking.


In the past month or so, I've been trying to convince myself to make the plan that was proposed in my "to do list". (It's been a few months since I found the "to do list" document with its plan, and a few months since the plan is to do all of it in an hour or so).


But I'm almost having trouble believing I'll actually do it before my next review (in January) and I'm only a month in, so it's hard to think that this is going to actually work for me. And yet I can't think of a better option (at this point) than just putting this thing off.


So I have a dilemma. I believe that not having acted upon my idea will cause the future of humanity to be worse off by at least an order of magnitude (probably more). And yet I won't do anything now. So why do I believe the future of humanity will be worse off by an order of magnitude? It's hard to explain this, I will try:


  • If I do anything now (even the smallest amount of), it will probably cause my life to turn into a series of "ifs, buts and maybes". I will probably still find a way to procrastinate on my own plan\xe2\x80\x94why should I believe that it won't influence my life in some other way?

  • If I don't act upon my idea now, the future of humanity might very well be worse off by a greater amount than if I had just wasted two years on this. So I should put off acting upon my idea.

Which side do I choose? I believe that the future of humanity (and perhaps all life) would be drastically worse off if I chose the "procrastinate on my idea" option. So I should do the opposite of that.


Maybe there's no good answer. I mean, I can't procrastinate on the question because I don't know how the future of humanity really is. And there's no good reason to believe that I have thought of everything that needs to be thought of. But it's also not really an opportunity to make a decision. So I go with the "do it now" strategy!


What do other people do about this dilemma? This would be useful to know. Or perhaps this question is a silly one! :)
