2 Why does it still _ahem _apply?


So when I think about my computer science coursework, the problem in the case above doesn't really apply. It's not a problem with "probability" being a bounded-minor-real-number, it's a problem about the concept "expected utility". In that setting, I expect you to show me at some point that, for any real number q between 0 and 1 (and I know my utility function increases in q), there exists some p\xe2\x88\x88(0,q) such that a certain probability distribution P\xe2\x80\xb2\xe2\x80\xb2(x) has a non-trivial expectation of the utility attained on x for any x. So that's the question you should be answering! If you don't know how to do that, then this problem is at least the sort of problem that you can get an outside view on, by looking at how math has turned out for me over my entire life.
